About BMR

What is BMR?

BMR is the number of calories your body needs to maintain basic life functions at complete rest.

Mifflin-St Jeor

Most accurate for the general population. Used as default.

Harris-Benedict

Older formula, tends to overestimate by 5-10%.

Your BMR is the starting point. Use the TDEE calculator to factor in your activity level.

How it's calculated

Mifflin-St Jeor: Men: 10×W + 6.25×H − 5×A + 5. Women: 10×W + 6.25×H − 5×A − 161.

Harris-Benedict: Men: 88.362 + 13.397×W + 4.799×H − 5.677×A. Women: 447.593 + 9.247×W + 3.098×H − 4.330×A.
